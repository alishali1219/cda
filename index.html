Impact

Although this project was created during a short design-a-thon sprint and did not go through development, it still had a meaningful impact on my growth as a designer. I practiced creating engaging financial literacy experiences, explored ways to gamify saving habits for millennials, and learned how to connect personal finance goals with collaborative community challenges. The process helped me strengthen my skills in UX research, rapid prototyping, and designing for user motivation. Most importantly, it inspired me to think about how design can make financial management feel approachable, empowering, and even fun.

Key Takeaways

Gamification can make overwhelming topics approachable

By turning financial tasks into progress-based challenges, we lowered emotional resistance.

Social motivation improves follow-through

Group islands encouraged accountability and made financial conversations less taboo.

Personalization increases relevance

Users were more likely to engage with financial literacy when it was tailored to their needs.
